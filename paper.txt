# Aqal-Instruct: Enhancing Urdu Mathematical Reasoning through Continued Pretraining and Instruction Tuning

## Abstract

Large Language Models (LLMs) have demonstrated strong reasoning capabilities in high-resource languages such as English. However, mathematical reasoning performance remains limited for low-resource languages, including Urdu, due to insufficient pretraining data and lack of high-quality reasoning datasets. In this work, we present **Aqal-Instruct**, an Urdu reasoning model developed through a two-stage adaptation pipeline. Starting from the Qwen-8B base model, we first perform continued pretraining on Urdu Wikipedia to improve language understanding and domain adaptation. Subsequently, we instruction-tune the model using approximately 6,000 Urdu Chain-of-Thought (CoT) mathematical reasoning examples derived from GSM8K. We evaluate Aqal-Instruct on the MGSM-Urdu benchmark consisting of 250 multilingual grade-school mathematics problems. Experimental results show that continued pretraining combined with Urdu reasoning instruction tuning substantially improves mathematical reasoning performance compared to existing Urdu-focused models such as Alif and Qalb. Our findings highlight the effectiveness of language adaptation followed by reasoning-centric instruction tuning for low-resource language reasoning tasks.

## 1 Introduction

Recent advances in Large Language Models (LLMs) have enabled significant improvements in natural language understanding and reasoning. Despite these achievements, most state-of-the-art models are optimized primarily for English and a handful of high-resource languages. Urdu remains underrepresented in both pretraining corpora and instruction-tuning datasets, limiting the performance of LLMs on reasoning-intensive tasks.

Mathematical reasoning serves as an important benchmark for evaluating a model's ability to perform multi-step logical inference. Benchmarks such as GSM8K and MGSM have become standard evaluation datasets for reasoning research. However, relatively little work has explored mathematical reasoning in Urdu.

To address this gap, we propose Aqal-Instruct, an Urdu reasoning model built through:

1. Continued pretraining on Urdu Wikipedia.
2. Instruction tuning on Urdu GSM8K Chain-of-Thought data.
3. Evaluation on MGSM-Urdu.

Our contributions are:

* Development of an Urdu-adapted reasoning model based on Qwen-8B.
* Construction and utilization of a 6K-example Urdu GSM8K CoT instruction-tuning dataset.
* Evaluation on MGSM-Urdu using automatic answer verification.
* Comparison against existing Urdu language models including Alif and Qalb.

## 2 Related Work

### 2.1 Large Language Models

Open-source LLMs such as Qwen, Llama, Mistral, and Gemma have demonstrated strong performance across a wide range of tasks. These models are typically trained on multilingual corpora but often exhibit weaker performance on low-resource languages.

### 2.2 Mathematical Reasoning

Datasets such as GSM8K and MGSM have become standard benchmarks for evaluating mathematical reasoning. Chain-of-Thought prompting has been shown to improve reasoning by encouraging intermediate step generation.

### 2.3 Urdu Language Models

Several Urdu-focused models have been proposed, including Alif and Qalb. While these models improve Urdu language understanding, their mathematical reasoning capabilities remain relatively unexplored.

## 3 Methodology

### 3.1 Overview

Aqal-Instruct is trained in two stages:

1. Continued Pretraining (CPT)
2. Supervised Fine-Tuning (SFT)

### 3.2 Base Model

We use Qwen-8B as the foundation model due to its strong multilingual capabilities and open-source availability.

### 3.3 Continued Pretraining

To improve Urdu language understanding, we perform continued pretraining on Urdu Wikipedia.

The objective is standard next-token prediction:

[
\mathcal{L} = - \sum_{t=1}^{T} \log P(x_t | x_{<t})
]

Continued pretraining allows the model to better capture Urdu vocabulary, syntax, and knowledge.

### 3.4 Urdu GSM8K Instruction Dataset

We construct an Urdu reasoning dataset consisting of approximately 6,000 examples translated and adapted from GSM8K.

Each example contains:

* Question
* Chain-of-Thought reasoning
* Final answer

Example format:

Question:
ایک دکان میں 5 سیب ہیں...

Reasoning:
پہلے تعداد معلوم کرتے ہیں...

Answer:
10

### 3.5 Instruction Tuning

After continued pretraining, we perform supervised fine-tuning using the Urdu GSM8K dataset.

Training examples follow a chat format:

User: <Question>

Assistant: <Chain-of-Thought> <Final Answer>

This stage teaches the model to generate step-by-step Urdu reasoning.

## 4 Evaluation

### 4.1 Benchmark

We evaluate on MGSM-Urdu, which contains 250 multilingual grade-school mathematics problems translated into Urdu.

### 4.2 Generation Protocol

For each benchmark question, the model generates a complete reasoning trace and final answer.

### 4.3 Automatic Answer Verification

Instead of exact string matching, we employ a semantic answer validation pipeline using GPT-OSS-20B.

The validator receives:

* Question
* Model solution
* Ground-truth answer

The validator determines whether the final numerical answer matches the expected answer.

Equivalent numerical forms are treated as correct:

* 2 = 2.0
* 5 = 5.00

The validator outputs only:

* True
* False

This approach reduces errors caused by formatting differences.

### 5.3 Error Analysis

Common failure cases include:

* Arithmetic mistakes
* Incorrect extraction of quantities
* Long reasoning chains with accumulated errors
* Ambiguous Urdu translations

Despite these challenges, Aqal-Instruct produces more coherent and accurate reasoning than baseline models.

## 6 Discussion

The results demonstrate that language adaptation and reasoning adaptation are complementary.

Continued pretraining improves Urdu comprehension, while instruction tuning on mathematical reasoning data teaches multi-step problem solving.

The gains observed on MGSM-Urdu suggest that relatively small reasoning datasets can substantially improve reasoning performance when combined with language-specific adaptation.

## 7 Limitations

Several limitations remain:

* Evaluation uses only 250 MGSM-Urdu examples.
* Automatic judging may introduce evaluation noise.
* The instruction dataset contains only 6K examples.
* The model is evaluated only on mathematical reasoning tasks.

Future work may explore larger Urdu reasoning corpora and broader reasoning benchmarks.

## 8 Conclusion

We introduced Aqal-Instruct, an Urdu reasoning model obtained through continued pretraining on Urdu Wikipedia followed by instruction tuning on a 6K-example Urdu GSM8K Chain-of-Thought dataset. Evaluation on MGSM-Urdu demonstrates improved mathematical reasoning performance compared with existing Urdu-focused models such as Alif and Qalb. Our findings indicate that combining language adaptation with reasoning-focused instruction tuning is an effective strategy for improving reasoning capabilities in low-resource languages.
